## SECTION 3: MUTUALLY ASSURED DESTRUCTION (MAD)

### Core Concept

**FACT:** MAD is doctrine of military strategy holding that full-scale nuclear use by attacker would result in complete annihilation of both attacker and defender.

**FACT:** MAD based on theory of rational deterrence - threat of strong weapons prevents enemy's use of same weapons.

**FACT:** Strategy is form of Nash equilibrium - once armed, neither side has incentive to initiate conflict or disarm.

### Game Theory Application

**FACT:** Thomas Schelling applied game theory to nuclear standoffs - deterrence depends on adversary's belief in credible threat.

**Analysis:** Credible threat requires:
1. Capability (weapons exist)
2. Knowledge (adversary aware of capability)
3. Will (belief that weapons would be used if attacked)
4. Survivability (second-strike capability ensures retaliation)

**FACT:** Because both Cold War sides had vast stocks + second-strike capability, any strike would lead to MAD - strong incentive to avoid nuclear war.

### Deterrence Effectiveness

**FACT:** MAD existence incentivized superpowers to engage in diplomatic efforts to limit arsenals - recognition that nuclear war yields no victors.

**Analysis:** The deterrence worked - no nuclear weapons used in warfare since 1945 despite multiple close calls (Cuban Missile Crisis, etc.).
