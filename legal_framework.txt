=== UK/EU LEGAL FRAMEWORK FOR ANALYSIS ===

1. GENERAL DATA PROTECTION REGULATION (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679
   Articles potentially violated:
   - Article 5: Principles relating to processing (lawfulness, fairness, transparency)
   - Article 6: Lawfulness of processing (consent, legitimate interests)
   - Article 7: Conditions for consent
   - Article 13: Information to be provided (transparency)
   - Article 21: Right to object to processing
   - Article 22: Automated decision-making and profiling
   - Article 25: Data protection by design and by default

2. DATA PROTECTION ACT 2018 (UK)
   - Part 2: General processing (mirrors GDPR)
   - Part 3: Law enforcement processing
   - Schedule 1: Special categories of personal data

3. PRIVACY AND ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS REGULATIONS (PECR) 2003
   - Regulation 6: Traffic data
   - Regulation 7: Location data
   - Regulation 8: Itemised billing
   - Regulation 21: Use of cookies and similar technologies

4. COMPUTER MISUSE ACT 1990
   - Section 1: Unauthorized access to computer material
   - Section 2: Unauthorized access with intent
   - Section 3: Unauthorized acts with intent to impair

5. CONSUMER RIGHTS ACT 2015
   - Part 1: Consumer contracts for goods, digital content and services
   - Section 34: Digital content to be of satisfactory quality
   - Section 36: Digital content to be fit for particular purpose

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY LAW
   - Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988
   - Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018
   - Common law breach of confidence

REMEDIES AVAILABLE:
- Compensation (GDPR Art. 82 - material and non-material damage)
- Injunctions to cease processing
- Data erasure (right to be forgotten)
- Criminal prosecution (Computer Misuse Act)
- IP damages (lost profits, unjust enrichment)
- Exemplary/punitive damages for flagrant violations

LIMITATION PERIODS:
- GDPR claims: Generally 6 years from date of knowledge
- Contract claims: 6 years from breach
- Personal injury (distress): 3 years from date of knowledge
- IP infringement: 6 years from infringement

JURISDICTION:
- UK courts (user located in UK)
- EU courts (GDPR applies to UK-based data subjects)
- ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) - regulatory enforcement
